
Kingdom Magic: The Dragon’s Fortress
Game Overview
Theme: Fantasy / Dragon’s Lair
Difficulty Level: Medium
Player Count: 2–6 players
Time Limit: 90 minutes
Age Range: 4th–6th graders (ages 9–12)
Location: 3rd Floor

Puzzle Flow (Room Sequence)
Room 1: The Restroom (Starting Room) — Players are locked in and must search for hidden clues and solve puzzles to unlock the door to the next room.
Room 2: Martin’s Room (The Study) — Players find a series of lock-and-key challenges and a multi-piece puzzle that reveals the password to the final room.
Room 3: Heidi’s Room (The Dragon’s Lair) — The final room where players must avoid waking the dragon and escape the fortress.

Game Master Notes
Briefing: The Game Master reads the lore (see Lore tab) aloud to players before the timer starts. Make sure players understand the rules: no climbing on furniture, no forcing locks, and ask for hints if stuck.

Clue System: Players may request up to 3 hints from the Game Master. Hints can be delivered on paper slipped under the door, through a walkie-talkie, or on a screen inside the room.

Reset Protocol: After each group, the Game Master must reset all props, re-lock all locks, replace any used invisible ink clues, and test all sensors (motion sensor in the Restroom, decibel sensor in the Dragon’s Lair) before the next group enters.

“Welcome, brave adventurers. The creatures of Kingdom Magic have captured you and locked you deep inside their ancient stone fortress. The walls around you are covered in strange, glowing symbols, and you can see scratch marks left behind by prisoners who came before you.

Some of those prisoners were very brave. They found a way out, and they left behind hidden clues to help the next group escape. Those clues are now your only hope.

But be warned — a great dragon is sleeping just beyond the iron door. You can hear it breathing. Its huge body rises and falls with every breath. It will not stay asleep forever. In exactly 90 minutes, the dragon will wake up, and there will be no way out.

The clock is ticking. Work together, search carefully, and stay sharp. Find the clues, solve the puzzles, and escape before time runs out.

Good luck — you are going to need it.”

Room 1: The Restroom (Starting Room)
Players begin the game locked inside a restroom on the 3rd floor of the fortress. The room is themed to look old and mysterious. Players must search the room, find hidden clues, and solve puzzles to unlock the door that leads to Room 2.

Puzzle Sequence
Puzzle 1 — Hidden Message (Search Puzzle): A clue is hidden behind the toilet. Players must search the room carefully to find it. This clue gives a hint for the next puzzle.

Puzzle 2 — Cabinet Search (Search Puzzle): Inside the cabinets, players will find another clue. The cabinets may be locked or simply closed, depending on setup.

Puzzle 3 — Invisible Ink (Reveal Puzzle): A secret message is written in invisible ink. Players must hold the message near the light bulb to reveal the hidden text. (Prop needed: UV or invisible ink pen and paper.)

Puzzle 4 — Math Challenge (Logic Puzzle): Players solve a math problem to get a number code. This code unlocks the next step. The math should be at a 4th–6th grade level (for example: multi-step word problems, fractions, or order of operations).

Puzzle 5 — Shower Mechanism (Mechanical Puzzle): Players must figure out how to turn on the shower using the faucet in the correct way. Turning the faucet to the right setting triggers the next clue to appear. (Note: Design the shower mechanism so it is safe and simple — no real water needed.)

Props List
Faucet (rigged with puzzle mechanism)
Towel (may hide a clue or key)
Toothbrush (decorative or hides a small clue)
Mirror (may reflect a hidden message or reveal UV ink)
Invisible ink pen and paper
Cabinets (with or without lock)
Math puzzle printout

Tech and Special Effects
Motion Sensor: A motion sensor is placed in the room to simulate fortress guards. If players move too quickly or step into a certain area, an alarm sound plays or a light flashes, adding tension and excitement. This is a scare effect only — it does not end the game.

Room 3: Heidi’s Room (The Dragon’s Lair)
This is the final room. Players enter the Dragon’s Lair — a dark, mysterious room where a sleeping dragon guards the exit. The dragon is a large prop (or bed made to look like a dragon). Players must be quiet and clever to find the key to the exit without waking the dragon. If they are too loud, the dragon “wakes up” (triggered by a decibel sensor), adding a scare effect.

Puzzle Sequence
Puzzle 1 — Mirror Clue (Search Puzzle): Outside the window, a small mirror is placed. Players must look through or angle the mirror to read a hidden clue on the wall or ceiling. This clue tells them where to find the exit key.

Puzzle 2 — The Hidden Key (Search Puzzle): A key is hidden under the dragon prop (or under the bed that looks like a dragon). Players must carefully search under the dragon without making too much noise.

Puzzle 3 — Escape the Lair (Final Challenge): Players use the key to unlock the exit door and escape the fortress. When the door opens, the game is complete — players have escaped!

Tech and Special Effects
Decibel Sensor: A decibel (sound level) sensor is placed near the dragon. If the room gets too loud, the dragon “wakes up” — this can trigger red flashing lights, a roaring sound effect, or both. This is a scare effect to add excitement. The Game Master can adjust the sensitivity so it is fair for the age group. The sensor does not end the game but adds a time penalty or extra challenge.

Props List
Dragon prop or themed bed
Small mirror (mounted outside window or on wall)
Hidden clue (written on wall or ceiling, visible only with mirror)
Exit key (hidden under dragon/bed)
Decibel sensor
Red flashing lights (triggered by sensor)
Dragon roaring sound effect speaker
Exit door with lock

Room 2: Martin’s Room (The Study)
After escaping the Restroom, players enter Martin’s Room — a study filled with books, a bed, and locked cabinets. This room has a series of lock-and-key puzzles that build on each other. The final puzzle in this room reveals the password to enter the Dragon’s Lair (Room 3).

Puzzle Sequence
Puzzle 1 — Under the Bed (Search Puzzle): Players search under the bed and find a key. This key opens the cabinets.

Puzzle 2 — Number Lock (Combination Lock): The cabinets contain a locked box with a combination number lock. The combination code may be hidden in a book on the shelf, or players may have picked up a clue from the Restroom that gives the code. Players enter the correct numbers to open the lock and retrieve the key inside.

Puzzle 3 — Jigsaw Puzzle (Multi-Piece Puzzle): Inside the cabinets, players find puzzle pieces. When all pieces are put together, the completed puzzle reveals a hidden message or image. When the puzzle is finished, a compartment opens (this may use a magnetic switch or software-controlled lock — consult with Boda for the tech setup).

Puzzle 4 — The Final Password (Code Puzzle): The opened compartment holds the final password. Players use this password to unlock the door to the Dragon’s Lair (Room 3).

Props List
Bed (with hidden key underneath)
Cabinets (locked, opened by key)
Combination number lock
Books (one contains the combination code)
Jigsaw puzzle pieces
Hidden compartment (magnetic or software-controlled release)
Password card or scroll
